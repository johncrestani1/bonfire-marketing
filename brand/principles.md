# Principles

Operating rules derived from the Doctrine. These guide every product decision, marketing message, and business choice.

## Build Principles

1. **One terminal, not thirteen tools.** We will never become a multi-app ecosystem. One install. One interface. Everything runs through the terminal.

2. **Ship compiled software, not SaaS.** Bonfire is a binary that installs on your machine. 35 quality gates. EV code-signed. If it doesn't run without internet (on local models), it doesn't ship.

3. **Local by default, cloud by choice.** The default path is always local. Cloud providers are opt-in, per-task, user-initiated. The terminal never phones home without explicit permission.

4. **Phone commands, desktop executes.** The primary interface is messaging apps on your phone. The desktop terminal is the engine room — it runs in the background. If a workflow requires sitting at a desktop, we failed.

5. **Provider-agnostic architecture.** Every AI provider is a swappable module. Adding a new provider should never require changing the core. No provider gets preferential integration.

## Marketing Principles

6. **Name the enemy.** Dashboard Slavery. Subscription Hell. Cloud Dependence. We name what we're fighting. We don't hedge with "some might prefer cloud." We state our position.

7. **Use real numbers.** $16,500/month for 13 outsourced roles. $2,400/year in AI subscriptions. 79% freelancer spend collapse. No "up to" or "as much as." Exact figures, cited sources.

8. **Sell the future, not the features.** The One-Person Empire. The general managing an AI army. The ghost agency. We sell what becomes possible, not what the software does.

9. **One sentence answers.** Every person in the company — engineering, sales, affiliates, support — must be able to answer "What is Bonfire?" in one sentence. The same sentence. If we can't, we've failed at category definition.

## Business Principles

10. **Own, don't rent.** $5,000 one-time. No subscriptions. No "freemium to paid" funnel. No monthly drain. The customer owns the software the day they pay.

11. **Whitelabel is a feature, not a threat.** AIDE-certified operators rebrand and resell Bonfire. This expands the market, not the competition. The rising tide lifts the fleet.

12. **Affiliates are partners, not channels.** 20% commission. Real assets. Real landing pages. Real support. Affiliates succeed because the product is worth $5,000, not because the commission is high.
