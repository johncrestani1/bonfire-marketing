# The Opportunity

## The One-Person Empire

A single entrepreneur can operate with the output of an entire company.

Bonfire provides the command center.

## The math

| The old way | The Bonfire way |
|---|---|
| 13 outsourced roles | 1 person + local AI |
| $16,500-37,000/month | $5,000 once |
| Data on 8 different vendor servers | Data on your machine |
| 14 logins, 6 subscriptions, 3 dashboards | 1 terminal, commands from your phone |
| You work FOR your tools | Your tools work for YOU |

## Who captures this opportunity

**Marketers** who run client campaigns and are tired of paying agencies that use the same AI tools they could run themselves.

**Content creators** who produce daily across video, audio, text, and social — and are drowning in tool subscriptions and manual workflows.

**Coaches** who manage client data, session recordings, and course material — and need it to stay private, not sitting on someone else's cloud.

## Why now

Three forces are converging:

1. **Local AI models are good enough.** TinyLlama, Whisper, and FFmpeg run on hardware from 2013. You don't need a data center.

2. **Cloud AI pricing is escalating.** Every major provider has raised prices in the last 12 months. The "free tier → dependency → price increase" playbook is playing out across the industry.

3. **Data privacy regulation is tightening.** Creators feeding client content into cloud AI are accumulating legal liability they haven't quantified.

The entrepreneurs who move into the one-person empire model now — while the incumbents are still raising prices and the market hasn't caught on — will have an insurmountable head start.

## The window

This opportunity has a shelf life.

Right now, most entrepreneurs don't know they can run AI locally. The cloud providers spend billions making sure of that. Every "free tier" is designed to create dependency before the real pricing kicks in.

The window for first-movers is open. It won't stay open.
