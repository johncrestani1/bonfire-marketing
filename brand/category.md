# Category Definition

## What is Bonfire Terminal?

**Bonfire Terminal is the local AI terminal that runs your entire marketing operation from your own machine.**

That's it. One sentence. Everything else derives from this.

## Breaking it down

**Local** — runs on your hardware, not someone else's cloud. Your data never leaves your device.

**AI** — speech-to-text, image editing, video creation, text generation, ad copy, content production.

**Terminal** — a command center, not a chatbot. You issue commands through your phone's messaging apps. The terminal executes on your desktop.

**Your entire marketing operation** — video editing, social media, email copy, ad creative, transcription, project coordination, data analysis. What used to require 13 outsourced roles.

**From your own machine** — one install, one investment, permanent ownership.

## What category do we own?

**Local AI Terminal.**

Not "AI tool." Not "automation platform." Not "chatbot." Not "SaaS."

A terminal. On your machine. That runs AI for your business.

This is a new category. Nobody else owns it. The competitors are in adjacent categories:
- Cloud AI suites (ChatGPT, Claude) — cloud, subscription, data leaves your machine
- Automation platforms (Zapier, Make, n8n) — connect APIs, not AI-native
- AI agent platforms (Lindy, Manus, OpenClaw) — cloud-hosted agents, not local
- Outsourced agencies — humans doing the work, $10K-37K/month

Bonfire sits at the intersection of all four and belongs to none of them.
