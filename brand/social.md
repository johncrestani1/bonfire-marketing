## PLATFORM 1: YOUTUBE

### Format Specifications

| Format | Duration | Aspect Ratio | Resolution |
|--------|----------|-------------|------------|
| Tutorial | 10-20 min | 16:9 | 1920x1080 |
| Hot Take | 8-15 min | 16:9 | 1920x1080 |
| Case Study | 12-20 min | 16:9 | 1920x1080 |
| Reactive/Trending | 5-10 min | 16:9 | 1920x1080 |

### Thumbnail Rules
- Dark background (#0d0d0d or similar)
- Large text (3-5 words max, Orbitron or Impact-weight sans-serif)
- One face, one emotion (surprise, intensity, concern)
- Fire gradient accent on key text
- No clutter -- one clear visual hierarchy

### Hook Structure (First 8 Seconds)
Every video opens with ONE of these patterns:
1. **Stat bomb**: "Uber burned their entire AI budget in four months." (data shock)
2. **Confession**: "I was spending $3,200 a month on tools I barely used." (personal vulnerability)
3. **Challenge**: "Open your browser. Count your tabs. Now count your subscriptions." (interactive)
4. **Demonstration**: "Watch this. One command. Done." (immediate proof)
5. **Contrarian**: "Everyone's talking about AI automation. They're wrong." (pattern interrupt)

### Posting Cadence
- 2 videos per week
- Tuesday + Thursday publish
- Mix: 1 tutorial/case study + 1 hot take/trending per week
- Shorts published separately (see Short-Form section)

### YouTube-Specific SEO
- Title: Under 60 characters, keyword-front-loaded
- Description: First 2 lines = hook (visible before "show more"). Include timestamps, links, keyword paragraph.
- Tags: "bonfire terminal," "local AI," "AI for entrepreneurs," "one person business AI," category-specific tags
- End screen: Always point to next video + subscribe
- Pinned comment: Key takeaway + CTA link

## PLATFORM 2: TWITTER/X

### Format Specifications

| Format | Length | Structure |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Thread | 8-15 tweets | Hook + body + CTA |
| Single tweet | 1 tweet (280 chars) | One stat or one take |
| Quote tweet | 1 tweet | React to industry news |
| Poll | 1 tweet + 4 options | Engage on doctrine topics |

### Thread Structure Template
```
Tweet 1 (Hook):
[Stat, confession, or contrarian statement]

(thread)

Tweets 2-N (Body):
One idea per tweet.
Short sentences.
Line breaks between thoughts.
Data with source when possible.

Final Tweet (CTA):
Restate the thesis in one sentence.
Link or call to action.
"Follow for more [topic]" or "RT if this changed your thinking."
```

### Posting Cadence
- 2 threads per week (Mon + Wed)
- 1-2 single tweets per day (observations, data points, hot takes)
- Quote tweets on breaking AI news (reactive, same-day turnaround)
- 1 poll per week (engagement driver)

### Voice Rules for X
- Short sentences. Paragraphs of 1-2 sentences max per tweet.
- No hashtags in threads (they reduce reach on X in 2026).
- Numbers get engagement. Lead with data when possible.
- Contrarian framing outperforms agreement framing.
- First tweet of a thread should be screenshot-worthy (people share screenshots).

### Thread Adaptation Guide (from content.md)

**Framework/Tactical Threads (TW-01 through TW-10)**
- These are the backbone. Each one teaches something actionable.
- Format: Hook tweet + 8-12 body tweets + CTA tweet.
- Include a visual (screenshot, table, or diagram) in tweet 3 or 4 to boost engagement.
- Republish the hook tweet as a standalone single tweet 48 hours later (different audience sees it).

**Data-Driven Threads (TW-11 through TW-18)**
- Lead with the single most shocking number.
- Source every claim. "Source: [name], [year]" at the end of the tweet.
- These threads get bookmarked and shared. Optimize for saves, not likes.
- Add a "save this thread" prompt in the final tweet.

**Contrarian/Provocative Threads (TW-19 through TW-25)**
- These will get disagreement. That is the point.
- Do NOT soften the hook. Polarization drives reach.
- Always have evidence in tweets 2-4 to back the provocative hook.
- End with a reframe, not a fight. "I'm not against [X]. I'm for [Y]."

### Poll Ideas (Weekly)
1. "How many AI subscriptions do you currently pay for?" (1 / 2-3 / 4-6 / 7+)
2. "If OpenAI doubled their price tomorrow, would you switch providers?" (Yes immediately / Yes eventually / No / I'd just pay)
3. "What's your biggest frustration with AI tools?" (Cost / Privacy / Reliability / Output quality)
4. "Could you run your business with zero employees if you had the right AI tools?" (Already do / Could if I had the tools / No way / Working on it)

## PLATFORM 3: SHORT-FORM VIDEO (TikTok / Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts)

### Format Specifications

| Platform | Max Duration | Aspect Ratio | Resolution | Captions |
|----------|-------------|-------------|------------|----------|
| TikTok | 60 sec (optimal: 15-30) | 9:16 | 1080x1920 | Required (auto-captions ON) |
| Instagram Reels | 90 sec (optimal: 15-30) | 9:16 | 1080x1920 | Required (burned in) |
| YouTube Shorts | 60 sec (optimal: 15-45) | 9:16 | 1080x1920 | Optional (auto-captions) |

### Hook Rules (First 2 Seconds)
Short-form lives or dies in the first 2 seconds. Every video must open with ONE of:
1. **On-screen text that creates curiosity**: "Your AI company doesn't want you to see this"
2. **Direct address to camera**: "How many dashboards do you have open right now?"
3. **Visual pattern interrupt**: Split screen, fast zoom, unexpected action
4. **Sound trigger**: Trending audio that stops the scroll

### Script Structure (15-30 second videos)
```
[0-2 sec] HOOK: Text on screen or spoken line. Stop the scroll.
[2-8 sec] PROBLEM: Name the pain point in 1-2 sentences.
[8-20 sec] SOLUTION: Show the terminal / the result / the transformation.
[20-30 sec] PAYOFF: The number, the contrast, the "now you know."
```

### Script Structure (30-60 second videos)
```
[0-2 sec] HOOK: Pattern interrupt.
[2-10 sec] CONTEXT: Set up the situation or problem.
[10-35 sec] DEMONSTRATION: Screen recording, side-by-side, or process walkthrough.
[35-50 sec] RESULT: Show the finished output or the data.
[50-60 sec] CTA: Follow, comment, or "link in bio."
```

### Posting Cadence
- 5 short-form videos per week (Mon through Fri)
- Cross-post to all 3 platforms (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
- Batch film: Record 10-15 hooks in one session, edit and schedule across 2-3 weeks
- Reuse trending audio within 48 hours of it trending

### Platform-Specific Adaptations

**TikTok**
- Skews younger but business TikTok is growing fast among 25-40 entrepreneurs
- Trending audio is CRITICAL -- use it even for business content
- Duets and stitches with popular AI/business creators for reach
- Comment engagement: Reply to top comments with follow-up videos
- Hashtags: #AItools #solopreneur #entrepreneur #onepersonbusiness #dashboardslavery

**Instagram Reels**
- Audience skews slightly older (28-45) and more polished
- Burned-in captions required (many watch on mute)
- Cover image matters -- choose a frame with clear text overlay
- Share Reels to Stories for additional reach
- Use carousel posts to repurpose thread content visually

**YouTube Shorts**
- Feeds directly into YouTube channel subscribers
- Can link to full-length YouTube videos ("Watch the full breakdown")
- Algorithm rewards consistency -- post daily if possible
- Title = hook text (appears below the video)

## PLATFORM 4: BLOG / SEO

### Format Specifications

| Type | Word Count | Structure |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Comparison/Review | 2,500-4,000 | H1 > intro > sections with H2s > comparison table > verdict |
| How-To Guide | 2,000-3,500 | H1 > intro > numbered steps with H2s > summary |
| Data-Driven Analysis | 2,000-3,000 | H1 > thesis > evidence sections > conclusion |
| Manifesto/Thought Leadership | 3,000-5,000 | H1 > narrative intro > doctrine sections > call to action |

### SEO Structure Template
```
H1: [Primary keyword] -- [Benefit or hook]
Meta description: [155 chars, includes primary keyword, ends with CTA or intrigue]

Intro (150-200 words):
- Open with the pain point or data hook
- State the thesis
- Preview what the reader will learn

H2: [Subtopic 1 -- secondary keyword]
[300-500 words, includes data, examples, screenshots]

H2: [Subtopic 2]
[300-500 words]

...

H2: Conclusion / What This Means for You
[200-300 words, restate thesis, CTA]
```

### Publishing Cadence
- 2 articles per week
- Monday (evergreen/guide) + Thursday (trending/reactive)
- Update top-performing articles quarterly with fresh data
- Internal linking: every article links to at least 2 other articles

## PLATFORM 5: EMAIL NEWSLETTER

### Format Specifications

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Frequency | 2x per week (Tuesday + Saturday) |
| Length | 500-800 words (3-4 minute read) |
| Format | Plain text preferred (higher deliverability), minimal HTML |
| From name | "John @ Bonfire" or "John Crestani" |
| Subject line | Under 50 characters, no ALL CAPS, no spam triggers |
| Preview text | First 90 characters -- treat as a second hook |

### Email Structure Template
```
Subject: [hook -- curiosity, data, or confession]
Preview: [extends the hook, doesn't repeat it]

---

[Opening line -- 1 sentence, personal or data-driven]

[2-3 paragraphs of content: story, insight, or framework]

[Key takeaway in bold or set apart]

[CTA -- one clear action: read, watch, reply, or buy]

---

[Sign-off: "-- John" or "Talk Tuesday, John"]

P.S. [Secondary CTA or teaser for next email]
```

### Subject Line Patterns That Work
1. **Confession**: "I added up my subscriptions. I almost threw up."
2. **Data hook**: "The 33:1 ratio that's killing freelancers"
3. **Curiosity gap**: "Your AI company just changed their terms. Did you notice?"
4. **How-to**: "How I make a week of content in 90 minutes"
5. **Free value**: "The Ghost Agency playbook (free)"
6. **News reaction**: "Uber burned through their AI budget in 4 months"
7. **Question**: "The question I get asked most (and my honest answer)"
8. **Validation**: "Apple just validated everything we've been saying"
9. **Niche-specific**: "A coach's secret: AI-generated session prep in 3 minutes"
10. **Philosophy**: "The one metric that matters for your AI setup"

### Segmentation (by audience)
| Segment | Emphasis | Email IDs to Prioritize |
|---------|----------|------------------------|
| Marketers | Cost savings, client deliverables, Ghost Agency | EM-01, EM-04, EM-05, EM-14 |
| Content Creators | Batch production, video pipeline, multi-platform | EM-04, EM-11, EM-12, EM-14 |
| Coaches | Client management, session prep, scaling without hiring | EM-09, EM-02, EM-07, EM-14 |
| Prospects (pre-purchase) | Price justification, comparison, social proof | EM-07, EM-13, EM-01, EM-15 |
| Customers (post-purchase) | Workflows, tips, advanced usage, community | EM-04, EM-09, EM-11, EM-14 |

### Email-to-Content Pipeline
Every email should include ONE of:
- Link to a YouTube video published that week
- Link to a blog post published that week
- Embedded Twitter thread link
- Direct reply prompt ("Hit reply and tell me...")

This creates a flywheel: email drives traffic to content, content drives email signups, replies drive engagement signals.

One piece of content becomes many. Here is how each YouTube video repurposes:

```
1 YouTube Video (15 min)

  +-- 1 Twitter/X thread (extracted key points)
  |
  +-- 2-3 Short-form clips (best 15-30 sec segments)
  |
  +-- 1 Blog post (expanded with SEO optimization)
  |
  +-- 1 Email newsletter (condensed version + link to video)
  |
  +-- 3-5 Single tweets (individual stats or quotes from the video)
  |
  +-- 1 LinkedIn post (professional angle, 200-300 words)
```
