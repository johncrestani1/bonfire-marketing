# The Bonfire Manifesto

---

## I.

I built a business that Forbes profiled. $500,000 a month. From a laptop. No office. No employees. Just me, an internet connection, and the willingness to figure it out.

Then I started using AI.

Not the way most people use it — asking ChatGPT to write a blog post. I was running an operation. Client campaigns. Brand voice documents. Ad copy at scale. Proprietary strategies that took years to develop.

And I was pasting all of it into someone else's server.

Every prompt. Every voice note. Every client deliverable. Sent to OpenAI. Sent to Anthropic. Sent to Google. Processed on hardware I don't own, in data centers I can't audit, under terms of service I didn't read until it was too late.

In December 2025, X/Twitter quietly rewrote their terms to claim perpetual, royalty-free rights to every AI prompt and output. No opt-out. In September 2025, LinkedIn auto-enrolled a billion users into AI training. They're now facing a class-action.

I was feeding the machine that would eventually compete with me using my own work.

That's when I stopped.

---

## II.

I didn't stop using AI. I stopped renting it.

I spent a year building something different. A terminal that runs on my machine. Speech-to-text that processes locally. Image editing that never touches a server. Text generation from a model installed on my own hardware.

Not a browser tab. Not a SaaS subscription. Not another dashboard to check every morning.

A terminal. On my desktop. That does what I tell it to do, from my phone, without asking anyone's permission.

I called it Bonfire.

---

## III.

You are being robbed.

Not by criminals. By subscriptions. By dashboards. By vendors who hold your data and charge you rent to access your own work.

You opened your laptop to create. Instead you spent three hours in dashboards. Checking analytics. Updating spreadsheets. Switching between eight tabs. Logging into fourteen different tools. Copying data from one screen to paste into another.

You are leashed to your computer. Leashed to subscriptions you forgot you had — the average business spends $7,900 to $17,000 per employee per year on SaaS, and 47% of those licenses go completely unused. Leashed to vendors who raise prices because they know you can't leave.

This is Dashboard Slavery. And it is the default operating mode of every entrepreneur who builds their business on someone else's infrastructure.

---

## IV.

There is another way.

One person. One terminal. One machine.

The AI runs on your hardware. Your phone is the remote control. Your desktop is the engine room. You send a command from Telegram while walking your dog, and by the time you get home, the video is edited, the transcription is done, and the email draft is waiting.

No agency. No freelancers. No thirteen outsourced roles at $16,500 a month. Freelancer marketplace spending has collapsed 79% since 2021. The smart operators didn't wait for the market to tell them. They built the alternative themselves.

This is the One-Person Empire. And the command center is Bonfire Terminal.

---

## V.

We believe:

**AI is the workforce. You're the boss.** AI amplifies entrepreneurs. It does not replace them. You direct. AI executes. The human is always in command.

**Your phone is the remote. Your desktop is the engine.** You should not be leashed to a laptop to run your business. Commands flow from your phone. Results return to your phone. The terminal runs in the background.

**No single vendor owns your future.** Local models, OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, Grok — you choose. You switch. When OpenAI's next price increase hits, you shrug. Provider agnosticism is insurance.

**If it leaves your device, it's not yours anymore.** Your data. Your clients' data. Your competitive edge. It stays on your machine or it's already gone. This is not a feature. It is the architecture.

**You're the general. AI is the army.** The next generation of businesses won't hire thirteen people. They'll hire one person and give them a terminal. The question is whether you'll be the general or one of the thirteen getting replaced.

---

We didn't build Bonfire Terminal to compete with ChatGPT.

We built it to make ChatGPT unnecessary.

For the coach whose session recordings sit on three different cloud platforms, none of which they actually control.

We built it for the entrepreneur who opened a business to build something — and instead got a full-time job managing dashboards.
