# The Future

## What happens next

The one-person empire is not a prediction. It's already happening.

The entrepreneurs who figured it out first aren't announcing it. They're not writing blog posts about their "AI strategy." They're running ghost agencies — operations that produce the output of a 13-person team, managed by one person, powered by AI that runs on their own hardware.

From the outside, it looks like they have a full team. They don't. They have Bonfire Terminal and a phone.

## The three waves

**Wave 1 (now): Early operators discover local AI.**
These are marketers, creators, and coaches who were already spending $200+/month on cloud AI and $10K+/month on outsourcing. They found Bonfire, ran the math, and switched. They're not evangelists. They're operators. The advantage is quiet.

**Wave 2 (6-12 months): The outsourcing correction.**
As more operators adopt local AI terminals, the outsourcing market contracts. Agencies that compete on labor arbitrage — "we'll do it cheaper than hiring in-house" — lose to operators who do it for $5,000 once. The smart agencies adopt Bonfire themselves and rebrand as AIDE-certified providers.

**Wave 3 (12-24 months): The one-person empire is normalized.**
Running a business alone with AI assistance becomes the default, not the exception. The question shifts from "can one person do all this?" to "why would you hire 13 people?"

## Where Bonfire sits

Bonfire Terminal is the command center for all three waves.

Wave 1 operators use it to replace their current tool stack.
Wave 2 agencies use it (via AIDE certification) to compete against their own disruption.
Wave 3 entrepreneurs start with it from day one — they never knew the old way.

The product evolves. The category doesn't change. Bonfire is the local AI terminal.
