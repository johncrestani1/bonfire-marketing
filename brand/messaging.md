## Message Architecture

Every piece of content maps to one of these three frames:

### Frame 1: The Problem (Dashboard Slavery)
You are leashed to your computer. Leashed to subscriptions. 14 logins, 8 dashboards, 6 recurring charges. You opened your laptop to create — instead you spent 3 hours managing tools.

**Use when**: cold traffic, awareness stage, top-of-funnel content, social hooks

### Frame 2: The Opportunity (The One-Person Empire)
One person can run an entire marketing operation from their phone. 13 outsourced roles replaced. $16,500/month in agency costs eliminated. The AI runs on your machine. You command from wherever you are.

**Use when**: warm traffic, consideration stage, webinar content, email nurture

### Frame 3: The Vehicle (Bonfire Terminal)
A local AI terminal. 16 bundled tools. 6 messaging bridges. Runs on your hardware. $5,000 once. No subscriptions. No cloud. 35 quality gates per release. Built by the guy Forbes profiled.

**Use when**: hot traffic, decision stage, sales pages, strategy call prep

## Objection Map

| Objection | Real concern | Response frame |
|---|---|---|
| "It's too expensive" | Risk of wasting $5K | ROI math: $16,500/mo outsourced → $5K once. Pays for itself in 10 days of agency savings. |
| "I'm not technical" | Fear of complexity | You send a Telegram message. That's the interface. If you can text, you can use Bonfire. |
| "I've been burned by AI tools" | Trust deficit | Bonfire is compiled software, not a SaaS wrapper. EV code-signed. 35 quality gates. Runs locally — verify everything yourself. |
| "I need to think about it" | Unresolved sub-objection | "Is it the investment, or something else?" Isolate the real blocker. |
| "Can I try it first?" | Want proof before commitment | Webinar demos. Case studies. The $27 entry point proves the methodology before the $5K commitment. |
| "What if it doesn't work for my niche?" | Specificity concern | "What messaging apps do your clients use?" If Telegram, Discord, WhatsApp, or Slack → it works. |

## Voice Rules (from brand/glossary.md)

- **Say**: terminal, command, local, own, machine, execute, operate, fire, build, ship
- **Don't say**: platform, solution, leverage, synergy, ecosystem, empower, disrupt, revolutionary
- **Tone**: direct, technical but accessible, confident, transparent
- **Readability**: Grade 7-8 for marketing, Grade 9-10 for technical docs
- **Headings**: Orbitron, ALL-CAPS, letter-spacing 2-4px
- **Body**: Rajdhani, sentence case, clean and sharp
